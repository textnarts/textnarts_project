The act of changing a website's language, appearance, and operation for markets that speak other languages is known as localization, also referred to as L10n. The webpage that results from localization will:

  • considers linguistic, cultural, and technical distinctions
  • does not alter the source text's original intent
  • feels and reads like it was written in the intended language from the start.

The eventual outcome of a website translation project—a website accessible to millions of potential new customers—might initially seem daunting, but it's essential for any company's long-term success.

Website localization is a more comprehensive procedure that involves adapting web material for consumption by a targeted group. This often entails translation, formatting and usability changes, as well as taking into account specific cultural preferences.

It is insufficient to merely translate your material from one language to another as your company grows internationally. You must change it so that it appeals to your clients in various markets, cultures, and settings.
